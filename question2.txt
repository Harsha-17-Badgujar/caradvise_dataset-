Q: What is included in a standard car maintenance service?

A: A standard car maintenance service typically includes an oil change, tire rotation, inspection of brakes, fluids, filters, belts, hoses, and other components, as well as a general check-up of the vehicle.

Q: How often should I have my car's wheels aligned?

A: It's recommended to have your car's wheels aligned every 12,000 to 15,000 miles, or if you notice uneven tire wear, steering pull, or other alignment issues.

Q: Why is it important to replace my cabin air filter?

A: The cabin air filter helps to clean the air that comes into the car's cabin, removing pollutants and allergens that can affect your health and comfort. A dirty or clogged cabin air filter can also reduce the efficiency of your car's heating and air conditioning system.

Q: When should I have my car's battery checked?

A: It's recommended to have your car's battery checked at least once a year, especially if it's more than 3 years old. Signs of a weak or dying battery include slow engine cranking, dim headlights, and other electrical issues.

Q: What is the recommended tire pressure for my car?

A: The recommended tire pressure can vary depending on the make and model of your car, as well as the type of tire you have. You can find the recommended tire pressure in your car's owner's manual, on a sticker inside the driver's door, or online. It's important to check your tire pressure regularly and keep it at the recommended level to ensure proper handling, fuel efficiency, and tire life.


Q: How often should I change my car's oil?

A: The general recommendation is every 5,000 to 7,500 miles or every six months, whichever comes first. However, you should refer to your car's owner manual for the manufacturer's recommended oil change interval.

Q: How can I tell if my car's brakes need to be replaced?

A: Signs that your brakes need to be replaced include squeaking or grinding noises when you apply the brakes, a soft or spongy brake pedal, or if the vehicle pulls to one side when you apply the brakes. If you experience any of these symptoms, it's important to have your brakes checked by a professional mechanic.

Q: Do I need to rotate my car's tires?

A: Yes, tire rotation is important for even wear and prolonging the life of your tires. You should have your tires rotated every 5,000 to 7,500 miles or as recommended by the tire manufacturer.

Q: What is a tune-up and how often do I need one?

A: A tune-up is a maintenance service that typically includes replacing spark plugs, ignition wires, and air and fuel filters. The frequency of a tune-up depends on your car's make, model, and age, but typically it's recommended every 30,000 to 100,000 miles.

Q: How often should I check my car's fluids?

A: You should check your car's fluids at least once a month, including engine oil, transmission fluid, brake fluid, coolant, and power steering fluid. Always refer to your car's owner manual for specific fluid type and level recommendations.

Q: What is a timing belt and when should it be replaced?

A: A timing belt is a component that synchronizes the rotation of the engine's camshaft and crankshaft. It's recommended to replace a timing belt every 60,000 to 100,000 miles or as recommended by the manufacturer, as a broken timing belt can cause serious engine damage.

Q: How often should I replace my car's air filter?

A: You should replace your car's air filter every 15,000 to 30,000 miles, or as recommended by the manufacturer. A dirty air filter can reduce engine performance and fuel efficiency.

Q: Should I use synthetic or conventional oil in my car?

A: It depends on your car's make and model, as well as your driving habits and climate. Synthetic oil offers better performance and protection in extreme temperatures, while conventional oil is generally less expensive. Refer to your car's owner manual for the manufacturer's recommended oil type.

Q: How often should I have my car's battery checked?

A: It's recommended to have your car's battery checked every six months, especially if it's more than three years old. A weak battery can cause starting and charging problems, and can ultimately fail unexpectedly.

Q: Can I perform car maintenance myself or should I go to a professional service center?

A: It depends on your level of knowledge and experience with car maintenance. Simple tasks like checking fluid levels, changing a tire, and replacing wiper blades can be done by most car owners. However, more complex maintenance tasks like timing belt replacement or engine repairs should be left to a professional mechanic.

A: Tires should be rotated every 5,000 to 7,500 miles to ensure even wear.

Q: How often should I replace my brake pads?

A: The brake pads should be replaced when they reach their minimum thickness, which can vary depending on the vehicle and driving conditions.

Q: How often should I replace my spark plugs?

A: It's typically recommended to replace spark plugs every 30,000 to 100,000 miles, depending on the type of spark plug.

Q: When should I replace my serpentine belt?

A: The serpentine belt should be replaced when it shows signs of wear or damage, or according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule, which can range from 60,000 to 100,000 miles.

Q: How often should I replace my engine coolant?

A: The engine coolant should be replaced according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule, typically every 2 to 5 years.

Q: How often should I get my brakes checked?

A: Brakes should be inspected at least once a year or whenever there are signs of problems such as noise or reduced braking performance.

Q: When should I replace my battery?

A: The battery should be replaced when it shows signs of weakness or after 3 to 5 years, depending on the type of battery.

Q: How often should I get my exhaust system checked?

A: The exhaust system should be checked every 50,000 miles or whenever there are signs of problems such as excessive noise or reduced performance.

Q: When should I replace my shocks and struts?

A: Shocks and struts should be replaced when they show signs of wear or damage, typically every 50,000 to 100,000 miles.
